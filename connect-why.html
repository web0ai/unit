Why check-ins work

The science and thinking behind intentional couple communication.

50 years of relationship science

The research is clear — structured communication isn't just nice to have, it's the foundation of lasting relationships.

Dr. John Gottman's 50 years of research found that couples who maintain a 5:1 ratio of positive to negative interactions have dramatically stronger relationships. Structured check-ins are one of the most effective ways to build this ratio consistently.

Gottman Institute

Gottman recommends a weekly 'State of the Union' conversation — a structured check-in where both partners share appreciations and address unresolved issues. Couples who do this report higher satisfaction and lower conflict.

Gottman Method

The best founding teams don't avoid conflict — they manage it with weekly syncs. The same principle applies to couples: consistent, structured communication prevents small misalignments from becoming relationship-ending conflicts.

Making it work

Four principles that make check-ins effective, not just routine.

1
📵
No phones. Close the laptop. Give each other 15–60 uninterrupted minutes.
2
📅
Same time every week. Consistency matters more than perfection.
3
🤝
Start with appreciation. Always. It sets the tone for everything that follows.
4
💬
If something comes up, note it — don't solve it mid check-in. Return to it after.
